Goat Struck by Lorry — Appears in Owner’s Dream to Say Thank You for the Food

By Dzulhussain Danish · Date 9 Jun 2026 · Category Sensational
🌐 Baca dalam Bahasa Malaysia BM

MELAKA – Sometimes, the stories that touch our hearts do not come from humans, but from animals.

A goat farmer from a village in Melaka claims to have received an unexpected visit in his dreams, several days after one of his prized goats was killed after being struck by a lorry.

According to the man, who wishes to be known only as Pak Din, the goat was no ordinary farm animal.

From when it was young, he had raised the white male goat himself, and it would often follow him wherever he went around the orchard.

“If I went behind the house, it would follow. To the orchard too. Sometimes I'd sit down for my afternoon tea and it would come and stand right beside me.” — Pak Din

The Accident on the Village Road

The incident occurred in the early morning when the goat was believed to have escaped from its pen.

According to several villagers, a lorry passing along the main road near the area was unable to swerve in time before striking the animal.

The goat died at the scene.

Pak Din admitted he was quite affected by the loss.

“People always say a goat is just livestock. But when you've raised it for years, it finds a place in your heart.” — Pak Din

According to family members, Pak Din appeared downcast for several days after the incident and would often sit staring into the distance on the front porch at night.

The Strange Dream

But what made this story the talk of the village happened three nights after the goat's death.

Pak Din claims he dreamed he was in a vast, green field. In the middle of that field stood a white goat he knew very well.

“In the dream it looked so healthy. Clean coat, plump body like when it was young.” — Pak Din

What shocked him more was that he claims the goat spoke.

According to Pak Din, the animal said a few words he still remembers to this day: “Thank you for feeding me. Thank you for taking care of me.”

Pak Din said that after hearing those words, the goat slowly walked toward a bright light before disappearing from sight. He then woke from his sleep.

Coincidence or Something More?

The story then spread among the villagers.

Some believe the dream was merely a reflection of an owner's grief over a beloved animal. To them, when a person has been that close to an animal they have cared for over many years, dreaming about it after its passing is perfectly normal.

But others believe the dream carried a deeper meaning.

An elderly villager noted that in many Malay cultural traditions, dreams involving a pet that has passed away are often regarded as a symbol of gratitude, or as closure to the bond between human and animal.

Whether true or not, no one can say for certain.

He Still Keeps the Goat's Collar

Notably, to this day Pak Din still keeps the collar the goat used to wear.

He says it is not because he cannot let go, but as a memento of a creature that was once a part of his daily routine.

“When I woke up in the morning, it was there. When I came home in the evening, it was there. After it was gone, things got a little quiet.” — Pak Din

He admits he does not know whether the dream truly held a special meaning, or was merely the wanderings of sleep.

But one thing is certain — after that dream, he no longer felt quite so sad.

As though something that had not yet been said was finally, in its own way, delivered.

And perhaps, in a way we ourselves do not fully understand, a goat simply wanted to say thank you one last time.

Psychological Analysis: Why Do We Dream of Those Who Are Gone?

From a psychological standpoint, dreams like the one Pak Din experienced are not unusual.

According to several studies on grief and loss, individuals who share a close emotional bond with a person or another living being often experience dreams involving them after death or separation.

Psychologists suggest that such dreams may be the mind's way of processing unresolved grief. During sleep, the brain reorganises memories, emotions, and experiences from daily life. In that process, a person can experience dreams that feel extraordinarily vivid.

Interestingly, the themes of “thank you,” “goodbye,” or “I am alright” are among the most commonly reported motifs in dreams connected to loss. Some researchers view these as a psychological mechanism that helps a person reach emotional closure.

In Pak Din's case, feelings of guilt at being unable to prevent the accident may also have played a role. The dream of the goat expressing gratitude could be interpreted as his subconscious reducing sorrow and accepting that he had done his best in caring for the animal.

That said, psychology does not necessarily dismiss such an experience as “just a dream.” Many psychologists acknowledge that even when dreams have a scientific explanation, the meaning a person attributes to their dream remains deeply significant.

For Pak Din, whether it was a message from beyond or simply the mind processing loss, the effect was the same — he woke the next morning with a heart far more at peace than the night before.
