“Zombie Attack at Night” Video — A Science School Student’s Thursday Night Project

By Iskandar Aizat · Date 10 Jun 2026 · Category Investigations

REMBAU — After years of being one of the most enduring legends among students and alumni, the “Zombie Attack at Night” video that once sent shockwaves through a Science Secondary School has finally received an official explanation from the individual responsible for producing it.

And yes — the “zombie” was not a zombie.

It was simply a Form 4 student willing to crawl along a road at 11 PM in the name of quality content.

The video, filmed on a Thursday night around 2021, shows a dark figure moving in an unsettling manner along the school road. With lighting that was virtually nonexistent and a camera shaking as though it too was gripped by maximum-level fear, the video succeeded in terrifying many students at the time.

According to a group of former students who recently came forward, the video was in fact nothing more than a spontaneous project dreamed up out of boredom one evening.

“We were just hanging around, and then someone thought it would be fun to make a horror video. One person was the cameraman, one became the zombie, and the rest played the victims who were supposedly shocked,” said one of the group's members.

What made it even funnier was that they had absolutely no expectation the video would be taken seriously by so many people.

“We thought people would just laugh. Turns out plenty of them were genuinely convinced there was a real zombie in the school.” — The Person Responsible

The student who played the zombie also admitted that the filming process was far less straightforward than it seemed.

“I had to crawl several times because I kept laughing. My knees hurt for two days. That was my artistic sacrifice.” — The Person Responsible

Not long after the video spread, all manner of theories began to emerge among the school community. Some claimed the filming location became noticeably quieter afterwards. Others said they had spotted the same figure several days later. And some devoted themselves entirely to frame-by-frame video analysis as though they were investigating an international crime case.

After the true identity of the “zombie” was finally revealed, many alumni could only laugh at how a zero-budget video had managed to sustain a mystery for nearly four years.

Although the secret is now out, the “Zombie Attack at Night” video remains one of the most legendary pieces of content ever produced by students of that school.

Only one question remains a mystery to this day.

Who was the first person to suggest crawling along the road at 11 PM?
