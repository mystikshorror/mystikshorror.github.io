Drove a Clapped-Out Car and it Broke Down — An Obese Ghost Had Hitched a Ride

By Iskandar Aizat · Date 9 Jun 2026 · Category Sensational
🌐 Baca dalam Bahasa Malaysia BM

BUKIT DINDING, SELANGOR — A young man experienced something he will not soon forget when the run-down car he was driving suddenly broke down in the middle of the road late at night. More disturbingly, he claims an enormous “mystery passenger” had hitched a ride uninvited.

According to the victim, who wishes to be known only as Man, the incident occurred while he was on his way home after attending a kenduri in the neighbouring village.

“To be honest, my car was already on its last legs. The door needed a kick to close, and the window required a very specific three-tap technique to wind up. But that night was different.” — Man

While passing along a quiet road near an old rubber plantation, the engine suddenly died. After several failed attempts to restart it, Man heard a sound like someone breathing heavily from the back seat.

At first he assumed it was coming from a flat tyre. But when he looked behind him, he saw a large figure sitting there, chewing on something.

“I couldn't tell if it was a ghost or a buffet regular. Its body filled the entire back seat. I just recited whatever I could remember and pretended I hadn't seen anything.” — Man

According to Man's account, the creature simply sat there in silence, occasionally sighing, causing the already battered car to lean noticeably to the left.

Villagers who heard the story had various theories. Some believed it was a resting holiday spirit (hantu raya), while others claimed it might have been the “obese ghost” known to haunt the area.

One elderly villager remarked, “There have been sightings of a large figure by the roadside before. But it doesn't usually bother people. It just looks for vehicles with broken shock absorbers.”

More amusingly, after the creature apparently vanished without explanation, Man's car started again without issue.

“Maybe it just needed a lift to the junction up ahead. I'm only upset it didn't chip in for petrol,” said Man, laughing.

Although the story has yet to be verified, villagers have advised the public to ensure their vehicles are in good working order before travelling at night.

“If a car already looks like a walking piece of scrap metal, it's not just people who won't trust it — even a ghost might take pity and decide to hitch along,” said one resident.

For now, the true identity of the “obese ghost” remains a mystery.
