Back from Camping, Child Plays Marbles with a Jinn Child — “Little sibling wants to play with little sibling’s friend.”

By Sarjan Bahrom · Date 10 Jun 2026 · Category Paranormal
🌐 Baca dalam Bahasa Malaysia BM
Think this article should be shared? 𝕏 / Twitter WhatsApp Copy Link

RAUB, PAHANG. A family of three — Encik Kamarul, Puan Haslinda, and their son Johan (not his real name) — returned to Peninsular Malaysia from Lahad Datu, Sabah, for a holiday after a long time away from extended family. Encik Kamarul is a General Operations Force (PGA) police officer originally from Jerantut, Pahang, and his wife, Puan Haslinda, is a full-time homemaker. Together with their only child, they arrived in Pahang and were invited by relatives to go camping at a rural homestay near Lata Jarum.

Since their son Johan, then five years old, had never been camping before, the family decided to join in. The camp ran for three days and two nights. During the camp itself, nothing strange happened. It was a week after they returned to Sabah that Encik Kamarul and Puan Haslinda began to notice unusual behaviour in their son. At night, Johan would appear to be talking to someone that no one else could see. At first, the family paid it no mind, assuming he was simply playing with an imaginary friend. The day they truly began to suspect something foreign had entered their home was when Puan Haslinda spotted Johan sitting beside a woman inside the house, seen through the window that overlooked the corridor of the police barracks where they lived. “OI!” Puan Haslinda shouted, assuming a disturbed stranger had broken in — but when she rushed inside, there was no one beside Johan.

After that incident, Puan Haslinda immediately called Encik Kamarul. That same night, all three of them brought Johan to visit a orang pandai (a traditional healer) who lived in a village near the PGA base where Encik Kamarul served. According to the healer — who Encik Kamarul said was of Mindanao Malay descent — it was not one, not two, but three jinn children who had taken a liking to Johan and wished to bring him back to their realm. That very night, Johan was bathed in flower water by the healer, and afterwards a red cord was tied around his ankle. “Do not remove this cord,” the healer warned. The family returned home the following morning after the Subuh prayer. But the frightening events did not end there. In fact, after they came home, the strange occurrences only multiplied.

Johan kept asking where his “friends” were. What the healer had forgotten to mention was this: the red cord only prevented the three jinn from disturbing Johan directly — they were still in the house. Encik Kamarul, who often came home late at night, would regularly glimpse figures moving around inside. Puan Haslinda frequently heard the sounds of someone singing and marbles being rolled around in Johan's bedroom while he was at school. The disturbances grew more aggressive over time, until one day when the whole family went out together and returned to find their house in complete disarray, as if a small tornado had passed through. They did not enter the house. Encik Kamarul and Puan Haslinda rushed straight to the Mindanao healer's home — only to find him gone. Too afraid to return home, the three of them made their way to a nearby mosque. There, they encountered an ustaz who seemed to know, without being told, exactly what they were going through.

“Go home. Look for stones, river wood, or bones that the child may have picked up somewhere,” the ustaz told them. Encik Kamarul got up and left immediately, leaving Puan Haslinda and Johan with the ustaz at the mosque. He returned about half an hour later. In his hand was a small figurine made of bone. “This isn't his,” Encik Kamarul said. The ustaz recited something, then took the small figurine and threw it out of the mosque. “Whatever we can do, we do. The rest, only God can grant.”

From that day on, there were no more disturbances in their home.
